Watch absolutely anything, together —
no matter the distance.

Hirae syncs video playback, scrolling, and browsing across any site — including cross-origin embeds most sync tools can't touch — then puts chat, push-to-talk, and audio/video calls right alongside it. Same show, same moment, same feeling of being in the same room.

No complicated setup. Three steps.

1
Install Hirae
Load the extension in Chrome, then sign up or log in right from the popup — nothing else to configure.
2
Open anything, together
Pull up the same page — a streaming site, a video embed, anything with a <video> tag — and play/pause/seek/scroll stay in sync automatically on both sides.
3
Talk while you watch
Text chat, push-to-talk, or a full audio/video call, layered right over whatever you're watching. Connect automatically once you're both online, or the zero-setup manual way — your call.

Built for the distance, not around it.

Long-distance takes the small, ordinary things and makes them a whole coordination problem — even just watching something at the same time. Hirae exists so "come watch this with me" doesn't need a screen-share workaround or a third-party watch-party site that only works on one streaming service. It works wherever the video already is.

  • 🎬 Any site, any streaming service, any embed — sync doesn't care where the video lives.
  • 💬 Chat and calls layered directly over the page, so you're never tabbing away from each other.
  • 🔒 Peer-to-peer by design — once connected, your video and audio never touch a server.

And the moments stick around.

Hirae Memories is the shared archive for everything you watch together — no extra sign-up, same account as the extension.

📸
Capture the moment
One click during a session composites both cameras and the screen you're watching into a themed collage — polaroid, cozy grid, filmstrip, couple, or family.
✍️
Write a review together
When the credits roll, leave your take on what you just watched — both sides' reviews sit side by side on the same memory.
💜
Like, comment, relive
Every moment and review can be liked and commented on — a running, low-key feed of your shared history, not just a folder of photos.
🔒
Your rules on who sees it
Set each moment to just the two of you, your contacts, or public — decided the moment you save it, changeable any time after.
